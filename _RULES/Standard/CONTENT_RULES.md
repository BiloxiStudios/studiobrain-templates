# Content Rules

## World Consistency Guidelines

### Setting and Timeline
- **Era**: Near-future cyberpunk (2080s-2090s)
- **Technology**: Advanced AI, neural interfaces, biotech, but not post-scarcity
- **Society**: Corporate-dominated with underground resistance and street culture
- **Geography**: Urban mega-cities with distinct districts and corporate zones

### Tone and Atmosphere
- **Primary**: Professional tech-noir with human elements
- **Secondary**: Subtle humor and personal connections
- **Avoid**: Overly dark grimdark or cartoonish comedy
- **Style**: Detailed but accessible, immersive but not overwhelming

### Narrative Consistency
- **Character Arcs**: Respect established personalities and relationships
- **Location Continuity**: Maintain district characteristics and brand presence
- **Brand Identity**: Keep corporate cultures and market positions consistent
- **Faction Dynamics**: Honor established alliances and conflicts

### Content Quality Standards

#### Detail Level
- **Sufficient**: Enough detail for clear visualization and understanding
- **Balanced**: Not so much detail that it becomes overwhelming
- **Specific**: Use concrete examples rather than vague descriptions
- **Relevant**: Every detail should serve the narrative or world-building purpose

#### Language and Style
- **Professional**: Clear, well-structured prose appropriate for game content
- **Accessible**: Understandable without sacrificing sophistication
- **Consistent**: Maintain established voice throughout all content
- **Engaging**: Draw readers into the world and make them care about entities

#### Technical Requirements
- **Markdown**: Use proper formatting for headers, lists, links, and emphasis
- **Structure**: Follow established templates and patterns
- **Validation**: Ensure content passes automated quality checks
- **Cross-Reference**: Link to related entities using proper ID patterns

### Content Types and Guidelines

#### Descriptive Content
- Use sensory details (what you see, hear, smell, feel)
- Include both physical and atmospheric descriptions
- Balance static descriptions with dynamic elements
- Show character and culture through environmental details

#### Dialogue and Voice
- Reflect character personality and background
- Use appropriate tech-noir vernacular and corporate speak
- Maintain consistency with established speech patterns
- Include cultural and class distinctions in language

#### Technical and Lore Content
- Explain technology in accessible terms
- Maintain consistency with established capabilities
- Include both benefits and limitations of technologies
- Connect individual tech to broader world systems

### Review and Validation

#### Self-Check Questions
1. Does this content fit naturally within the City of Brains universe?
2. Are all entity relationships and references accurate?
3. Is the tone appropriate for the content type and context?
4. Does this add meaningful value to the overall narrative?
5. Will this content enhance player/reader engagement?

#### Common Issues to Avoid
- Contradicting established lore or relationships
- Overusing technical jargon without explanation
- Creating generic content that could fit any cyberpunk setting
- Ignoring established entity characteristics and relationships
- Making content too dark/serious or too light/comedic for context
