# City of Brains World Lore Rules

## The Retro-Futuristic 1990s Setting

### Timeline and Technology Paradox

#### Alternate 1990s Reality
City of Brains exists in a timeline where certain technologies advanced while others remained deliberately retro. Chemical processing and industrial development accelerated, while personal communication technology stagnated by design.

#### The BOL System Era
"Brain OnLine" serves as this world's internet equivalent - a dial-up network accessed through public terminals and home computers. The familiar AOL-style interface ("You've got brain!") masks a more limited but monitored communication system.

#### No Mobile Revolution
Cell phones never became mainstream, keeping society dependent on payphones and landlines. This limitation becomes crucial during the outbreak when communication infrastructure fails.

### Geographic and Corporate Structure

#### The Mountain Chemical Plant
The shadowy facility overlooking the region serves as the primary antagonist. This corporation dumps experimental waste into the water supply while maintaining a facade of legitimate industrial operation.

#### City Zone System
- **Central Nervous Park**: Neutral carnival/concert venue at the city's heart
- **Northside (Club Neuro)**: Tech-focused district with cyberpunk aesthetics
- **Westside (El Fighters)**: Desert trailer park culture with biker/trucker influence  
- **Eastside (Club Moon)**: Luxury district representing 90s excess and early influencer culture
- **Southside (Flamy's)**: Industrial zone with underground club scenes and fashion culture

#### The Town
Suburban starting area representing idealized 90s small-town America, slowly revealing cracks in the facade through environmental storytelling.

### Faction and Cultural Framework

#### Faction Territory Control
Each city zone is controlled by a distinct faction with its own culture, values, and aesthetic. Players can align with one faction, gaining benefits in that territory but hostility elsewhere.

#### Regional Brands and Corporations
- **Double Dip Dog Sauce**: The infection vector disguised as popular condiment
- **Snake Pit Pool Cleaners**: Local business exposing players to contaminated water
- **Chopy's Garage**: Southwest-influenced custom vehicle shop
- **Gear Head Tools**: Honest local tool manufacturer (rare in this universe)

#### Social Resistance Through Authenticity
Unlike cyberpunk corporate resistance, City of Brains features resistance through maintaining authentic human connections and practical skills in the face of chemical corporate control.

### The Infection Framework

#### Chemical Contamination Origins
The zombie outbreak stems from industrial pollution and experimental chemical additives, not supernatural or viral causes. This grounds the horror in environmental themes.

#### Eclipse Catalyst Effect
The lunar eclipse serves as both timekeeper and supernatural element, with the infection's spread tied to astronomical events and ancient folklore about "Blood Moons."

#### Persistence and Consequences
Unlike traditional zombie media, killed NPCs remain zombies permanently, creating lasting consequences for player actions and world state changes.

### Economic and Survival Systems

#### Dual Currency Structure
- **Gold**: Droppable, bankable currency used for consumables and essential items
- **BrainBucks**: Permanent account-bound currency for cosmetics and premium items

#### High-Stakes Resource Management
Players lose carried items and currency on death, making banking decisions and safe zone management crucial survival mechanics.

#### Skill-Based Progression
Real-world practical skills (plumbing, electrical work, mechanics) provide gameplay advantages and reflect the value of authentic knowledge over corporate training.

### Cultural and Tone Guidelines

#### Dark Humor Within Bounds
The universe maintains a T-rating through implication and subtext rather than explicit content. Corporate evil is suggested through environmental details and dialogue subtext.

#### 90s Nostalgia with Sinister Undertones
Familiar 90s elements (payphones, dial-up, physical media) are present but twisted to serve the narrative of corporate control and environmental disaster.

#### Environmental Storytelling Priority
Major plot points and corporate conspiracy elements are revealed through environmental details rather than exposition, rewarding observant players.
